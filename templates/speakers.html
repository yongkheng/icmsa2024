Keynote Speakers

Prof. Dr. Abdel Sahli
University of Essex, United Kingdom
" "

Professor Abdel Sahli holds the position of Professor of Operational Research within the School of Mathematics, Statistics, and Actuarial Science (SMSAS) at the University of Essex. With a foundation in operational research, applied mathematics, and computer science, his ongoing research encompasses optimization mathematical programming and heuristics, particularly focusing on evolutionary computing, nature-inspired algorithms, and the Strawberry algorithm. Professor Sahli imparts knowledge in nonlinear optimization and numerical analysis through his teaching endeavors. Additionally, he has provided supervision for over 10 PhD students.

Prof. Dr. Huang Huang-Nan, M.Si
Tunghai University, Taiwan
"Feature Selection in Echocardiogram Dataset using Machine Learning"

Currently serving as a full Professor and the Dean of the College of Science at Tunghai University in Taiwan, Professor Huang focuses his research on various areas, including biomedical signal and image processing, robust control theory, numerical analysis, delay systems, and fluid mechanics. He has garnered recognition for his achievements, receiving awards for his work in supervised and unsupervised learning on echocardiogram data, ensuring the data confidentiality of ECG signals, and developing wavelet-based watermarking and compression techniques for SCG signals, among other notable contributions.

Prof. Dr. Wong Hoi Ying
The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong
" "

Professor Wong Hoi Ying holds the position of Professor in the Risk Management Science Program within the Department of Statistics at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. He serves as an associate editor for the International Journal of Theoretical and Applied Finance and has authored several books, including "Simulation Techniques in Financial Risk Management" and "Handbook of Financial Risk Management: Simulations and Case Studies." Professor Wong's research focuses on derivatives pricing, interest rate modeling, financial risk management, and statistical finance.
